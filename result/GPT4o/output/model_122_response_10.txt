```modelica
model ClassicModel
  // Parameters
  parameter Real alpha = 0.1 "Reproduction rate of prey";
  parameter Real beta = 0.02 "Mortality rate of predator per prey";
  parameter Real gamma = 0.4 "General mortality rate of predator";
  parameter Real delta = 0.02 "Reproduction rate of predator per prey";

  // State variables
  Real x(start=10) "Prey population";
  Real y(start=10) "Predator population";

equation
  // Differential equations
  der(x) = alpha * x - beta * x * y;
  der(y) = delta * x * y - gamma * y;
end ClassicModel;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: These are constants that define the rates of reproduction and mortality for both prey and predators.
  - `alpha`: The reproduction rate of the prey.
  - `beta`: The mortality rate of the predator per prey.
  - `gamma`: The general mortality rate of the predator.
  - `delta`: The reproduction rate of the predator per prey.

- **State Variables**: These represent the populations of prey (`x`) and predators (`y`), with initial values set to 10 for both.

- **Equations**:
  - `der(x) = alpha * x - beta * x * y`: This equation models the change in prey population, considering its natural growth and reduction due to predation.
  - `der(y) = delta * x * y - gamma * y`: This equation models the change in predator population, considering its growth due to consuming prey and reduction due to natural mortality.

This model can be used to simulate and analyze the dynamics of predator-prey interactions over time, providing insights into ecosystem behavior and population control strategies.
