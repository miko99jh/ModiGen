Certainly! Below is a simple implementation of a `BouncingBall` model in Modelica. This model simulates a ball bouncing under the influence of gravity, with a specified coefficient of restitution for the bounce.

```modelica
model BouncingBall
  parameter Real radius = 0.1 "Radius of the ball";
  parameter Real e = 0.9 "Coefficient of restitution";
  parameter Real g = 9.81 "Acceleration due to gravity";
  parameter Real initialHeight = 1 "Initial height of the ball";
  parameter Real initialVelocity = 0 "Initial velocity of the ball";

  Real height(start = initialHeight) "Height of the ball";
  Real velocity(start = initialVelocity) "Velocity of the ball";

equation
  // Differential equation for velocity
  der(velocity) = -g;

  // Differential equation for height
  der(height) = velocity;

  // Event for bouncing
  when height <= radius then
    velocity = -e * velocity;
    reinit(height, radius); // Ensure the ball doesn't go below the ground
  end when;

end BouncingBall;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: 
  - `radius`: The radius of the ball.
  - `e`: The coefficient of restitution, which determines how much velocity is retained after a bounce.
  - `g`: The acceleration due to gravity.
  - `initialHeight`: The initial height from which the ball is dropped.
  - `initialVelocity`: The initial velocity of the ball.

- **Variables**:
  - `height`: The current height of the ball.
  - `velocity`: The current velocity of the ball.

- **Equations**:
  - The differential equation for `velocity` accounts for the acceleration due to gravity.
  - The differential equation for `height` updates the position based on the current velocity.

- **Event Handling**:
  - The `when` clause detects when the ball hits the ground (i.e., when `height` is less than or equal to `radius`).
  - Upon hitting the ground, the velocity is updated to simulate the bounce using the coefficient of restitution `e`.
  - The `reinit` function is used to reset the height to the radius to prevent the ball from going below the ground level.

This model will simulate the bouncing behavior of the ball, taking into account the effects of gravity and the bounce dynamics.
