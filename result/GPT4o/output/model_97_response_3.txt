To create a Modelica model for simulating the temperature change of a platinum resistance sensor, you can use the heat transfer equation to model the heat exchange between the sensor and the medium. The heat transfer is governed by the equation:

\[ \frac{dT_j}{dt} = \frac{\alpha \cdot A_r \cdot (T_g - T_j)}{c \cdot \rho \cdot V} \]

Here's how you can implement this in a Modelica model:

```modelica
model RTD_Pt100
  // Parameters
  parameter Real V = 1.15e-9 "Volume of the sensor (m^3)";
  parameter Real c = 456 "Comprehensive specific heat (J/kg.K)";
  parameter Real Ar = 5.31e-6 "Sensor surface area (m^2)";
  parameter Real alpha = 800 "Convective heat transfer coefficient (W/m^2.K)";
  parameter Real rho = 7980 "Comprehensive density (kg/m^3)";

  // Inputs
  input Real Tg "Temperature of the measured medium (°C)";

  // Outputs
  output Real Tj(start=25) "Temperature of the sensor (°C)";

  // Equation
  equation
    der(Tj) = (alpha * Ar * (Tg - Tj)) / (c * rho * V);
end RTD_Pt100;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: These are the physical properties of the sensor and the medium, such as volume, specific heat, surface area, heat transfer coefficient, and density.
- **Inputs**: `Tg` is the temperature of the medium, which is stable at 100°C in this scenario.
- **Outputs**: `Tj` is the temperature of the sensor, which starts at 25°C.
- **Equation**: The differential equation models the rate of change of the sensor's temperature over time due to the heat exchange with the medium.

This model will simulate how the sensor's temperature changes over time as it approaches the medium's temperature. You can simulate this model using a Modelica simulation environment to observe the sensor's temperature response over time.
