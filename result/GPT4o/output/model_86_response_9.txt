To implement a `BouncingBall` model in Modelica, you can follow the structure below. This model simulates a ball that falls under the influence of gravity and bounces upon hitting the ground. The coefficient of restitution is used to model the energy loss during the bounce.

```modelica
model BouncingBall
  // Parameters
  parameter Real radius = 0.1 "Radius of the ball in meters";
  parameter Real e = 0.9 "Coefficient of restitution";
  parameter Real g = 9.81 "Acceleration due to gravity in m/s^2";

  // State variables
  Real h(start = 1) "Height of the ball in meters";
  Real v(start = 0) "Velocity of the ball in m/s";

  // Event to detect collision with the ground
  Boolean isOnGround;

equation
  // Differential equation for velocity
  der(v) = if isOnGround then 0 else -g;

  // Differential equation for height
  der(h) = v;

  // Event condition for collision with the ground
  isOnGround = h <= radius and v <= 0;

  // When the ball hits the ground, reverse and reduce the velocity
  when isOnGround then
    reinit(v, -e * v);
    reinit(h, radius);
  end when;

  // Ensure the height does not go below the radius
  assert(h >= radius, "Ball has penetrated the ground!");

end BouncingBall;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: 
  - `radius`: The radius of the ball.
  - `e`: The coefficient of restitution, which determines how much velocity is retained after a bounce.
  - `g`: The gravitational acceleration.

- **State Variables**:
  - `h`: The height of the ball.
  - `v`: The velocity of the ball.

- **Equations**:
  - The differential equation for `v` accounts for gravity when the ball is not on the ground.
  - The differential equation for `h` is simply the velocity.
  - The `isOnGround` condition checks if the ball is touching the ground and moving downwards.

- **Events**:
  - When the ball hits the ground (`isOnGround` is true), the velocity is reversed and reduced by the coefficient of restitution, and the height is reset to the radius to simulate a bounce.

- **Assertions**:
  - An assertion is used to ensure the ball does not penetrate the ground.

This model should simulate the bouncing behavior of the ball, taking into account the energy loss during each bounce.
