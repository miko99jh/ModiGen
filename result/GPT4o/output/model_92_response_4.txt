Below is the implementation of the `PDController` model in Modelica. This model simulates the water level control system using proportional-differential (PD) control.

```modelica
model PDController
  // Parameters
  parameter Real Kp = 1 "Proportional gain";
  parameter Real Kd = 0.1 "Differential gain";
  parameter Real target_height = 1 "Target water level (m)";
  parameter Real initial_waterLevel = 0.2 "Initial water level (m)";
  parameter Real timeStep = 0.1 "Time step for simulation (s)";
  
  // Variables
  Real waterLevel(start = initial_waterLevel) "Current water level in the container";
  Real error "Difference between target and current water level";
  Real dError "Rate of change of error (derivative of error)";
  Real Pcontrol "Proportional control term";
  Real Dcontrol "Differential control term";
  Real controlSignal "Control signal (sum of Pcontrol and Dcontrol)";
  Real inflowRate "Rate of water inflow (m/s)";
  Real outflowRate = 0.05 "Constant outflow rate (m/s)";
  
equation
  // Error calculation
  error = target_height - waterLevel;
  
  // Proportional control
  Pcontrol = Kp * error;
  
  // Differential control (rate of change of error)
  der(error) = dError;
  Dcontrol = Kd * dError;
  
  // Control signal
  controlSignal = Pcontrol + Dcontrol;
  
  // Inflow rate is proportional to the control signal
  inflowRate = max(0, controlSignal); // Ensure inflow rate is non-negative
  
  // Water level dynamics
  der(waterLevel) = inflowRate - outflowRate;
  
  annotation(
    experiment(StartTime = 0, StopTime = 50, Tolerance = 1e-6, Interval = timeStep),
    Documentation(info = "PDController model to maintain water level at target height using proportional-differential control."));
end PDController;
```

### Explanation of the Model:
1. **Parameters**:
   - `Kp` and `Kd` are the proportional and differential coefficients, respectively.
   - `target_height` is the desired water level in the container.
   - `initial_waterLevel` is the starting water level in the container.
   - `timeStep` is the simulation time step.

2. **Variables**:
   - `waterLevel` represents the current water level in the container.
   - `error` is the difference between the target height and the current water level.
   - `dError` is the rate of change of the error (derivative of the error).
   - `Pcontrol` and `Dcontrol` are the proportional and differential control terms, respectively.
   - `controlSignal` is the sum of `Pcontrol` and `Dcontrol`, which determines the inflow rate.
   - `inflowRate` is the rate at which water flows into the container.
   - `outflowRate` is a constant rate at which water flows out of the container.

3. **Equations**:
   - The error is calculated as the difference between the target height and the current water level.
   - The proportional control term is directly proportional to the error.
   - The differential control term is proportional to the rate of change of the error.
   - The control signal is the sum of the proportional and differential terms.
   - The inflow rate is determined by the control signal, ensuring it is non-negative.
   - The water level changes based on the difference between the inflow and outflow rates.

4. **Simulation**:
   - The simulation runs from `StartTime = 0` to `StopTime = 50` seconds with a time step of `timeStep = 0.1` seconds.
   - The goal is to observe how the water level (`waterLevel`) approaches the target height (`target_height`) over time.

### How to Use:
1. Copy the code into a Modelica environment (e.g., OpenModelica or Dymola).
2. Simulate the model and plot the `waterLevel` variable to observe how it stabilizes at the target height of 1 meter.
