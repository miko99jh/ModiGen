Below is the implementation of the `PDController` model in Modelica. This model simulates the water level control system using proportional-differential (PD) control.

```modelica
model PDController
  // Parameters
  parameter Real Kp = 1 "Proportional gain";
  parameter Real Kd = 0.1 "Differential gain";
  parameter Real target_height = 1 "Target water level (m)";
  parameter Real initial_waterLevel = 0.2 "Initial water level (m)";
  
  // Variables
  Real waterLevel(start = initial_waterLevel) "Current water level (m)";
  Real error "Height error (m)";
  Real dError "Rate of change of error (m/s)";
  Real Pcontrol "Proportional control output";
  Real Dcontrol "Differential control output";
  Real controlSignal "Control signal (m/s)";
  
  // Time derivative of water level
  Real waterLevelRate "Rate of change of water level (m/s)";
  
equation
  // Error calculation
  error = target_height - waterLevel;
  
  // Proportional control
  Pcontrol = Kp * error;
  
  // Differential control
  der(error) = dError; // Derivative of error
  Dcontrol = Kd * dError;
  
  // Control signal
  controlSignal = Pcontrol + Dcontrol;
  
  // Water level dynamics
  der(waterLevel) = controlSignal;
  
  annotation(
    experiment(StartTime = 0, StopTime = 20, Tolerance = 1e-6, Interval = 0.1),
    Documentation(info = "PD Controller to maintain water level at target height."));
end PDController;
```

### Explanation:
1. **Parameters**:
   - `Kp`: Proportional gain.
   - `Kd`: Differential gain.
   - `target_height`: Desired water level in the container.
   - `initial_waterLevel`: Initial water level in the container.

2. **Variables**:
   - `waterLevel`: Current water level in the container.
   - `error`: Difference between the target height and the current water level.
   - `dError`: Rate of change of the error (derivative of the error).
   - `Pcontrol`: Contribution of proportional control.
   - `Dcontrol`: Contribution of differential control.
   - `controlSignal`: Combined control signal from proportional and differential control.
   - `waterLevelRate`: Rate of change of the water level.

3. **Equations**:
   - The error is calculated as the difference between the target height and the current water level.
   - The proportional control is directly proportional to the error.
   - The differential control is proportional to the rate of change of the error.
   - The control signal is the sum of the proportional and differential control outputs.
   - The rate of change of the water level (`der(waterLevel)`) is determined by the control signal.

4. **Simulation**:
   - The simulation runs from `StartTime = 0` to `StopTime = 20` seconds.
   - The water level is expected to approach the target height of 1 meter over time.

You can simulate this model in a Modelica environment (e.g., OpenModelica or Dymola) to observe how the water level changes over time.
