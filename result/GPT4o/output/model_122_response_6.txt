To create a Modelica model for simulating the dynamics of a predator-prey system, you can use the following code. This model includes the specified parameters and differential equations to represent the interactions between prey and predator populations.

```modelica
model ClassicModel
  // Parameters
  parameter Real alpha = 0.1 "Reproduction rate of prey";
  parameter Real beta = 0.02 "Mortality rate of prey per predator";
  parameter Real gamma = 0.4 "Natural mortality rate of predator";
  parameter Real delta = 0.02 "Reproduction rate of predator per prey";

  // State variables
  Real x(start = 10) "Prey population";
  Real y(start = 10) "Predator population";

equation
  // Differential equations
  der(x) = alpha * x - beta * x * y;
  der(y) = delta * x * y - gamma * y;
end ClassicModel;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: 
  - `alpha`: Reproduction rate of the prey.
  - `beta`: Mortality rate of the prey due to predation.
  - `gamma`: Natural mortality rate of the predator.
  - `delta`: Reproduction rate of the predator per prey consumed.

- **State Variables**:
  - `x`: Represents the prey population, initialized to 10.
  - `y`: Represents the predator population, initialized to 10.

- **Equations**:
  - `der(x) = alpha * x - beta * x * y`: The rate of change of the prey population is determined by its natural growth minus the losses due to predation.
  - `der(y) = delta * x * y - gamma * y`: The rate of change of the predator population is determined by the growth due to consuming prey minus its natural mortality.

This model can be used to simulate and analyze the dynamics of predator-prey interactions over time.
