```modelica
function SphereProperties
  input Real radius "Radius of the sphere";
  output Real surface "Surface area of the sphere";
  output Real volume "Volume of the sphere";
algorithm
  surface := 4 * Modelica.Constants.pi * radius^2;
  volume := (4/3) * Modelica.Constants.pi * radius^3;
end SphereProperties;
```

### Explanation:
- **Inputs and Outputs**: The function takes one input, `radius`, which is the radius of the sphere. It outputs two values: `surface` for the surface area and `volume` for the volume of the sphere.
- **Algorithm Block**: The calculations are performed within the `algorithm` block.
  - **Surface Area**: Calculated using the formula \(4 \pi r^2\).
  - **Volume**: Calculated using the formula \(\frac{4}{3} \pi r^3\).
- **Modelica.Constants.pi**: This is the constant for π provided by the Modelica standard library, ensuring precision and standardization in calculations.
