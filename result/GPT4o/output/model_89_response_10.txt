```modelica
model NewtonCoolingDynamic
  // Parameters
  parameter Real T0 = 100; // Initial temperature of water in °C
  parameter Real T_inf_initial = 25; // Initial ambient temperature in °C
  parameter Real A = 1; // Area in m²
  parameter Real m = 1; // Mass in kg
  parameter Real c_p = 4200; // Specific heat in J/(kg.°C)
  parameter Real h = 767; // Convection cooling coefficient in W/(m².°C)

  // Variables
  Real T(start = T0); // Temperature of water in °C
  Real T_inf; // Ambient temperature in °C
  Real Q_dot; // Heat transfer rate in W

initial equation
  T = T0; // Set initial temperature

equation
  // Ambient temperature changes after 20 seconds
  if time < 20 then
    T_inf = T_inf_initial;
  else
    T_inf = 0;
  end if;

  // Newton's law of cooling
  Q_dot = h * A * (T - T_inf);

  // Energy balance equation
  m * c_p * der(T) = -Q_dot;

end model NewtonCoolingDynamic
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: These are constants used in the model, such as initial temperature (`T0`), initial ambient temperature (`T_inf_initial`), area (`A`), mass (`m`), specific heat (`c_p`), and convection cooling coefficient (`h`).
- **Variables**: `T` is the temperature of the water, `T_inf` is the ambient temperature, and `Q_dot` is the heat transfer rate.
- **Initial Equation**: Sets the initial temperature of the water to `T0`.
- **Equations**:
  - The ambient temperature `T_inf` changes from `T_inf_initial` to 0°C after 20 seconds.
  - Newton's law of cooling is used to calculate the heat transfer rate `Q_dot`.
  - The energy balance equation relates the rate of change of temperature to the heat transfer rate.
