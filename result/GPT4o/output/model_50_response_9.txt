Here is the Modelica model for the `AD_DA_conversion` circuit as described:

```modelica
model AD_DA_conversion
  // Import necessary libraries
  import Modelica.Electrical.Analog.Ideal.AD_Converter;
  import Modelica.Electrical.Analog.Ideal.DA_Converter;
  import Modelica.Electrical.Analog.Sources.SineVoltage;
  import Modelica.Electrical.Analog.Basic.Resistor;
  import Modelica.Electrical.Analog.Basic.Ground;
  import Modelica.Electrical.Digital.Sources.Pulse;
  import Modelica.Electrical.Digital.Interfaces.Logic;

  // Components
  AD_Converter aD_Converter(
    N=7, 
    Rin=1e6, 
    VRefLow=0, 
    VRefHigh=10) "Analog-to-Digital Converter";

  DA_Converter dA_Converter(
    N=7, 
    Vref=10) "Digital-to-Analog Converter";

  SineVoltage sineVoltage(
    V=5, 
    f=10, 
    offset=5) "Sine wave voltage source";

  Resistor resistor(R=1) "Resistor for error measurement";

  Ground ground "Ground reference";

  Pulse pulse(
    pulse=Logic.'1', 
    quiet=Logic.'0', 
    nperiod=-1, 
    width=1, 
    period=0.001, 
    startTime=0) "Digital pulse generator";

equation
  // Connect pulse output to trigger inputs of AD and DA converters
  connect(pulse.y, aD_Converter.trig);
  connect(pulse.y, dA_Converter.trig);

  // Connect sine voltage source to AD converter
  connect(sineVoltage.p, aD_Converter.p);
  connect(sineVoltage.n, aD_Converter.n);

  // Connect digital output of AD converter to digital input of DA converter
  connect(aD_Converter.y, dA_Converter.x);

  // Connect ground to sine voltage and DA converter
  connect(sineVoltage.n, ground.p);
  connect(dA_Converter.n, ground.p);

  // Connect resistor between sine voltage source and DA converter output
  connect(sineVoltage.p, resistor.p);
  connect(dA_Converter.p, resistor.n);
end AD_DA_conversion;
```

### Explanation of the Model
1. **AD_Converter**: Converts the analog signal from the sine wave source into a digital signal. The parameters `N`, `Rin`, `VRefLow`, and `VRefHigh` are set to their default values.
2. **Pulse**: Generates a digital pulse signal to trigger the AD and DA converters. The pulse width, period, and start time are set to their default values.
3. **DA_Converter**: Converts the digital signal back into an analog signal. The parameters `N` and `Vref` are set to their default values.
4. **SineVoltage**: Generates a sine wave signal with an amplitude of 5 V, a frequency of 10 Hz, and a DC offset of 5 V.
5. **Resistor**: Measures the error between the original analog signal and the reconstructed analog signal.
6. **Ground**: Provides a common reference point for the circuit.

### Connections
- The pulse output is connected to the trigger inputs of both the AD and DA converters.
- The sine wave source is connected to the input of the AD converter.
- The digital output of the AD converter is connected to the digital input of the DA converter.
- The ground is connected to the negative terminals of the sine wave source and the DA converter.
- The resistor is connected between the sine wave source and the output of the DA converter to measure the error.

This model demonstrates the process of analog-to-digital and digital-to-analog conversion, including the reconstruction of the original analog signal and the measurement of any error introduced during the conversion process.
