It includes a parameter `T_amb` of type `Modelica.SIunits.Temperature` and a heat transfer interface `node` of type `Modelica.Thermal.HeatTransfer.Interfaces.HeatPort_a`.
```modelica
model AmbientCondition
  parameter Modelica.SIunits.Temperature T_amb = 293.15 "Ambient temperature in Kelvin";
  
  Modelica.Thermal.HeatTransfer.Interfaces.HeatPort_a node "Heat transfer interface";

equation
  node.T = T_amb;
  node.Q_flow = 0; // No heat flow into or out of the infinite reservoir

```

### Explanation:
- **Parameter `T_amb`:** This parameter represents the ambient temperature and is initialized to 293.15 K (20°C) by default. You can change this value as needed.
- **HeatPort_a `node`:** This is the heat transfer interface that connects to other thermal components. It allows the model to interact with other components in a thermal network.
- **Equation `node.T = T_amb`:** This equation ensures that the temperature at the heat port is always equal to the ambient temperature.
- **Equation `node.Q_flow = 0`:** This ensures that there is no heat flow into or out of the infinite reservoir, representing its infinite capacity to absorb or supply heat without changing temperature.
- **Annotation:** Provides a simple graphical representation of the model in a Modelica tool, with a rectangle and text indicating the model's purpose.
