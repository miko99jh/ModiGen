Certainly! Below is a Modelica model named `Damper` for a rotational damper. This model includes a parameter `d` representing the rotational damping constant and extends the `ModelicaByExample.Components.Rotational.Interfaces.Compliant` interface. The model implements the equation for the damping relationship, where the torque `tau` equals `d` multiplied by the derivative of `phi_rel`.

```modelica
model Damper
  extends ModelicaByExample.Components.Rotational.Interfaces.Compliant;

  parameter Modelica.SIunits.RotationalDampingConstant d "Rotational damping constant (Nm/(rad/s))";

equation
  tau = d * der(phi_rel);
end Damper;
```

This model is designed to simulate the damping effect in rotational systems, helping to mitigate oscillations and stabilize motion by applying a torque proportional to the rate of change of the relative angular displacement (`phi_rel`).
