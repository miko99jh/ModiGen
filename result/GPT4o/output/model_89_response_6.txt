```modelica
model NewtonCoolingDynamic
  // Parameters
  parameter Real T0 = 100; // Initial temperature of water in °C
  parameter Real T_inf_initial = 25; // Initial ambient temperature in °C
  parameter Real A = 1; // Area in m²
  parameter Real m = 1; // Mass in kg
  parameter Real c_p = 4200; // Specific heat in J/(kg.°C)
  parameter Real h = 767; // Convection cooling coefficient in W/(m².°C)

  // Variables
  Real T(start = T0); // Temperature of the water in °C
  Real T_inf; // Ambient temperature in °C
  Real Q_dot; // Heat transfer rate in W

initial equation
  T = T0; // Set initial temperature

equation
  // Newton's law of cooling
  Q_dot = h * A * (T - T_inf);
  der(T) = -Q_dot / (m * c_p);

  // Ambient temperature change after 20 seconds
  when time >= 20 then
    T_inf = 0;
  else
    T_inf = T_inf_initial;
  end when;
end NewtonCoolingDynamic;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: These are constants that define the initial conditions and properties of the system.
  - `T0`: Initial temperature of the water.
  - `T_inf_initial`: Initial ambient temperature.
  - `A`: Area through which heat is transferred.
  - `m`: Mass of the water.
  - `c_p`: Specific heat capacity of the water.
  - `h`: Convection cooling coefficient.

- **Variables**:
  - `T`: Temperature of the water, initialized to `T0`.
  - `T_inf`: Ambient temperature, which changes after 20 seconds.
  - `Q_dot`: Rate of heat transfer.

- **Initial Equation**: Sets the initial temperature of the water.

- **Equations**:
  - The heat transfer rate `Q_dot` is calculated using Newton's law of cooling.
  - The rate of change of temperature `der(T)` is determined by the heat transfer rate divided by the product of mass and specific heat capacity.
  - A `when` statement is used to change the ambient temperature `T_inf` from 25°C to 0°C after 20 seconds.

This model will simulate the cooling process of the water, taking into account the sudden drop in ambient temperature after 20 seconds.
