To create a Modelica model for simulating the temperature change of a platinum resistance sensor, you can use the following code. This model will calculate the temperature change of the sensor over time based on the heat balance between the sensor and the medium. The medium temperature (`Tg`) is defined as an input, and the sensor temperature (`Tj`) is defined as an output.

```modelica
model RTD_Pt100
  // Parameters
  parameter Real V = 1.15e-9 "Volume of the sensor (m^3)";
  parameter Real c = 456 "Comprehensive specific heat (J/(kg·K))";
  parameter Real Ar = 5.31e-6 "Sensor surface area (m^2)";
  parameter Real alpha = 800 "Convective heat transfer coefficient (W/(m^2·K))";
  parameter Real pho = 7980 "Comprehensive density (kg/m^3)";
  
  // Inputs
  input Real Tg = 100 "Temperature of the measured medium (°C)";
  
  // Outputs
  output Real Tj(start=25) "Temperature of the sensor (°C)";
  
  // Variables
  Real Q "Heat transfer rate (W)";
  Real m "Mass of the sensor (kg)";
  
equation
  // Calculate the mass of the sensor
  m = pho * V;
  
  // Heat transfer rate from the medium to the sensor
  Q = alpha * Ar * (Tg - Tj);
  
  // Heat balance equation
  der(Tj) = Q / (m * c);
  
end RTD_Pt100;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: These are constants that define the physical properties of the sensor and the environment.
  - `V`: Volume of the sensor.
  - `c`: Comprehensive specific heat.
  - `Ar`: Sensor surface area.
  - `alpha`: Convective heat transfer coefficient.
  - `pho`: Comprehensive density.

- **Inputs**: 
  - `Tg`: Temperature of the measured medium, which is stable at 100°C.

- **Outputs**: 
  - `Tj`: Temperature of the sensor, initialized at 25°C.

- **Variables**:
  - `Q`: Heat transfer rate from the medium to the sensor.
  - `m`: Mass of the sensor, calculated from its volume and density.

- **Equations**:
  - The mass of the sensor is calculated using its volume and density.
  - The heat transfer rate `Q` is calculated using the convective heat transfer equation.
  - The rate of change of the sensor's temperature `der(Tj)` is determined by the heat balance equation, which relates the heat transfer rate to the sensor's mass and specific heat.

This model will simulate how the sensor's temperature changes over time as it approaches the temperature of the medium. You can simulate this model in a Modelica environment to observe the sensor's temperature response.
