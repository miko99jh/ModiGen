```modelica
model ClassicModel
  // Parameters
  parameter Real alpha = 0.1 "Reproduction rate of prey";
  parameter Real beta = 0.02 "Mortality rate of predator per prey";
  parameter Real gamma = 0.4 "General mortality rate of predator";
  parameter Real delta = 0.02 "Reproduction rate of predator per prey";

  // State variables
  Real x(start=10) "Prey population";
  Real y(start=10) "Predator population";

equation
  // Differential equations
  der(x) = alpha * x - beta * x * y;
  der(y) = delta * x * y - gamma * y;
end ClassicModel;
```

### Explanation:
- **Parameters**: 
  - `alpha`: Reproduction rate of the prey.
  - `beta`: Mortality rate of the predator per prey.
  - `gamma`: General mortality rate of the predator.
  - `delta`: Reproduction rate of the predator per prey.

- **State Variables**:
  - `x`: Represents the prey population, initialized to 10.
  - `y`: Represents the predator population, initialized to 10.

- **Equations**:
  - `der(x)`: Represents the rate of change of the prey population. It increases by the reproduction rate (`alpha * x`) and decreases by the predation rate (`beta * x * y`).
  - `der(y)`: Represents the rate of change of the predator population. It increases by the reproduction rate due to predation (`delta * x * y`) and decreases by the natural mortality rate (`gamma * y`).

This model is a classic representation of the Lotka-Volterra equations, often used to study the dynamics of predator-prey interactions in ecological systems.
