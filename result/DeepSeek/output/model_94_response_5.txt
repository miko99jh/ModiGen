
Suppose we have a hydraulic system consisting of two oil storage tanks and a cylindrical connecting pipe. It is known that the density of oil (`rho`) in the tank is 0.2; The initial height of the liquid in the left tank (`H1`) is 0.4 and the cross-sectional area (`A1`) is 1; The initial height of the liquid in the right tank (`H2`) is 0.6 and the cross-sectional area (`A2`) is 2. The connection pipe has a length (`L`) of 0.1 and a diameter (`D`) of 0.2, and the dynamic viscosity (`mu`) of the oil is 2e-3. The model also calculates a flow coefficient (`c`) based on the pipe dimensions and viscosity. Please help me implement the modelica model named `TwoTanks` of this system, including equations for pressures (`P1`, `P2`), flow rate (`Q`), and the conservation of mass to determine the rate of change of the heights.

//modelica
```model TwoTanks
  parameter Real rho = 0.2; // oil density
  parameter Real H1 = 0.4; // initial height of oil in tank 1
  parameter Real A1 = 1; // cross-sectional area of tank 1
  parameter Real H2 = 0.6; // initial height of oil in tank 2
  parameter Real A2 = 2; // cross-sectional area of tank 2
  parameter Real L = 0.1; // length of pipe
  parameter Real D = 0.2; // diameter of pipe
  parameter Real mu = 2e-3; // dynamic viscosity of oil
  Real P1(start=1e5); // pressure in tank 1
  Real P2(start=1e5); // pressure in tank 2
  Real Q; // flow rate
  Real c; // flow coefficient
  Real dh1_dt; // rate of change of height in tank 1
  Real dh2_dt; // rate of change of height in tank 2
equation
  c = (pi*D^4)/(128*L*mu); // calculate flow coefficient
  Q = c*sqrt(abs(P1 - P2)); // calculate flow rate
  dh1_dt = -Q/A1; // conservation of mass for tank 1
  dh2_dt = Q/A2; // conservation of mass for tank 2
end TwoTanks;```

Note: The Modelica language is used for modeling and simulation. The code provided should be written in Modelica syntax.

The model should include the following equations:

1. The pressure in each tank (`P1`, `P2`) is calculated based on the height of the oil (`H1`, `H2`) in the tanks and the density of the oil (`rho`).
2. The flow rate (`Q`) is calculated based on the pressure difference between the two tanks and the flow coefficient (`c`).
3. The conservation of mass is applied to each tank to calculate the rate of change of the height (`dh1_dt`, `dh2_dt`).

The model should be named `TwoTanks`.

Please note that the pressure in each tank is calculated based on the height of the oil in the tank and the density of the oil. The pressure in a tank is calculated as the density of the oil multiplied by the gravitational acceleration (`g`) multiplied by the height of the oil in the tank. In this case, the gravitational acceleration is assumed to be 9.81 m/s^2.

The flow coefficient (`c`) is calculated based on the pipe dimensions and the dynamic viscosity of the oil. The flow coefficient is calculated as the ratio of the cross-sectional area of the pipe to the length of the pipe, multiplied by the square root of the ratio of the dynamic viscosity of the oil to the density of the oil.

The flow rate (`Q`) is calculated based on the pressure difference between the two tanks and the flow coefficient (`c`). The pressure difference is calculated as the absolute value of the difference between the pressure in tank 1 and the pressure in tank 2.

The conservation of mass is applied to each tank to calculate the rate of change of the height (`dh1_dt`, `dh2_dt`). The rate of change of the height in each tank is calculated as the negative of the flow rate divided by the cross-sectional area of the tank.
