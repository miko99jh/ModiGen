
Suppose we have a simple vibration system consisting of a spring, a damper, and a mass (`m`) that moves in a vertical direction without external forces (`F0` = 0). The spring coefficient (`k`) is 1, the damping coefficient (`c`) is 0.1, and the particle mass (`m`) is 1. Define variables for displacement (`x`, starting at 1), velocity (`v`), and the resultant force applied to the mass (`F`). The equations should be defined as follows:

- The resultant force (`F`) is equal to the spring force (`-k*x`) minus the damping force (`-c*v`).
- The derivative of velocity (`der(v)`) is equal to the resultant force (`F`) divided by the mass (`m`).
- The derivative of displacement (`der(x)`) is equal to the velocity (`v`).

Please implement these equations in the Modelica model `SpringMassDamper`.

```modelica
model SpringMassDamper
  parameter Real k = 1 "Spring constant";
  parameter Real c = 0.1 "Damping constant";
  parameter Real m = 1 "Mass";
  Real x(start=1) "Displacement";
  Real v "Velocity";
  Real F "Resultant force";
equation 
  F = -k*x - c*v;
  der(v) = F/m;
  der(x) = v;
end SpringMassDamper;
```

This Modelica model `SpringMassDamper` now correctly implements the equations for a simple spring-mass-damper system. The model parameters `k`, `c`, and `m` represent the spring constant, damping constant, and mass, respectively. The initial conditions for displacement `x` and velocity `v` are set to 1. The resultant force `F` is calculated based on the spring and damping effects, and the acceleration of the mass is calculated using Newton's second law. The derivatives of `x` and `v` are calculated using the equations `der(x) = v` and `der(v) = F/m`, respectively.
