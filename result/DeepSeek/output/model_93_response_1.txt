The PID control equation is given by:

```
u(t) = Kp * e(t) + Ki * integral(e(t) dt) + Kd * derivative(e(t) dt)
```

where `u(t)` is the control output, `e(t)` is the error signal, `Kp` is the proportional gain, `Ki` is the integral gain, and `Kd` is the derivative gain.

The integral term (`Icontrol`) is the integral of the error over time, and the derivative term (`Dcontrol`) is the rate of change of the error. The control output (`waterLevel`) is the sum of the proportional, integral, and derivative terms.

Please implement the model in Modelica using the given parameters and initial conditions.

```modelica
model PIDController
  parameter Real Kp=1;
  parameter Real Ki=0.2;
  parameter Real Kd=0.1;
  parameter Real target_height=1;
  parameter Real initial_waterLevel=0.2;
  Real waterLevel(start=initial_waterLevel);
  Real error;
  Real Pcontrol;
  Real Icontrol;
  Real Dcontrol;
  Real leakRate=0.1;
initial equation
  error = target_height - initial_waterLevel;
equation
  Pcontrol = Kp * error;
  Icontrol = Ki * integral(error * dt);
  Dcontrol = Kd * derivative(error * dt);
  waterLevel = Pcontrol + Icontrol + Dcontrol - leakRate;
end PIDController;
```

This Modelica model implements a PID controller to maintain the water level in the pool at a target height of 1 meter. The proportional, integral, and derivative terms are calculated based on the error between the current water level and the target height. The control output is the sum of these terms, minus the rate at which the water leaks out of the pool.
