
Create a Modelica model named `NewtonCooling` for Newton's law of cooling. Key parameters include ambient temperature (Temperature T_inf=300.0), initial temperature (Temperature T0=280.0), convective cooling coefficient (ConvectionCoefficient h=0.7), surface area (Area A=1.0), mass of thermal capacitance (Mass m=0.1), and specific heat capacity (SpecificHeat c_p=1.2). The output should be the temperature change over time, governed by the formula m*c_p*der(T) = h*A*(T_inf-T), depicting the cooling process. This model is commonly used in thermal analysis to understand the cooling dynamics of objects exposed to an environment with a different temperature.

//modelica
```model NewtonCooling
  parameter Real T_inf = 300.0 "Ambient temperature";
  parameter Real T0 = 280.0 "Initial temperature";
  parameter Real h = 0.7 "Convection coefficient";
  parameter Real A = 1.0 "Surface area";
  parameter Real m = 0.1 "Mass of thermal capacitance";
  parameter Real c_p = 1.2 "Specific heat capacity";
  Real T(start=T0) "Temperature";
equation
  m*c_p*der(T) = h*A*(T_inf-T);
end NewtonCooling;
```

This model uses Newton's law of cooling, which states that the rate of heat loss of a body is directly proportional to the difference in temperatures between the body and its surroundings. The formula m*c_p*der(T) = h*A*(T_inf-T) represents this law, where m is the mass of the object, c_p is the specific heat capacity, T is the temperature of the object, T_inf is the temperature of the surroundings, and h is the heat transfer coefficient.

The initial temperature T0 is set to 280.0, and the ambient temperature T_inf is set to 300.0. The convective cooling coefficient h is set to 0.7, the surface area A is set to 1.0, the mass of thermal capacitance m is set to 0.1, and the specific heat capacity c_p is set to 1.2.

The model's output is the temperature change over time, governed by the equation m*c_p*der(T) = h*A*(T_inf-T). This equation depicts the cooling process of the object, showing how the temperature of the object changes over time as it cools down in the environment with a different temperature.
