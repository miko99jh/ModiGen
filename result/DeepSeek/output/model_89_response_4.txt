
Please use modelica to help me implement a model named `NewtonCoolingDynamic`: Simulate the temperature change of a water under Newton's law of cooling. Among them, the initial temperature of water (`T0`) is 100 °C, the ambient temperature (`T_inf`) is 25 °C, the water meter area (`A`) is 1 square meter, the mass (`m`) is 1kg, the specific heat (`c_p`) is 4200 J/(kg.°C), and the convection cooling coefficient (`h`) is 767 W/(m2.°C). Use an initial equation to set `T` to `T0`. 20 seconds after the start of the simulation, the ambient temperature suddenly dropped to 0°C.

//modelica
```model NewtonCoolingDynamic
  parameter Real T0(unit="degC") = 100;
  parameter Real T_inf(unit="degC") = 25;
  parameter Real A(unit="m^2") = 1;
  parameter Real m(unit="kg") = 1;
  parameter Real c_p(unit="J/(kg.degC)") = 4200;
  parameter Real h(unit="W/(m^2.degC)") = 767;
  Real T(start=T0, unit="degC");
equation
  der(T) = (T_inf - T) / (m * c_p) + h * A * (T_inf - T) / (m * c_p);
  when time = 20 "s" then
    T_inf = 0 "degC";
  end when;
end NewtonCoolingDynamic;
```

This model describes the Newton's law of cooling, which states that the rate of heat loss of a body is directly proportional to the difference in temperature between the body and its surroundings. The model uses the following parameters:

- `T0`: The initial temperature of the water in degrees Celsius.
- `T_inf`: The ambient temperature in degrees Celsius.
- `A`: The surface area of the water in square meters.
- `m`: The mass of the water in kilograms.
- `c_p`: The specific heat capacity of the water in J/(kg.°C).
- `h`: The convection cooling coefficient in W/(m2.°C).

The model also includes an initial equation that sets `T` to `T0`. After 20 seconds, the ambient temperature suddenly drops to 0°C. The model uses a when-statement to change the value of `T_inf` at that time.

Please note that the model assumes that the water is in a perfectly insulated container, so there is no heat loss or gain through the container walls.
