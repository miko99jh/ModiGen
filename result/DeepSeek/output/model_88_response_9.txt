  parameter Real R(unit="Ω") = 100;

  Real V(start=0, unit="V");
  Real i_L(start=0, unit="A");
  Real i_R(start=0, unit="A");
  Real i_C(start=0, unit="A");

  // Add your equations here

1. Kirchhoff's current law (KCL): The sum of currents entering a node is zero. In this case, the sum of `i_L`, `i_R`, and `i_C` equals zero.

2. KVL: The sum of the voltages around a loop is zero. In this case, `Vb` equals `V` + `L*der(i_L)` + `R*i_R` + `1/C*integral(i_C, time)`.

3. Capacitor current: `i_C` equals C*der(V).

4. Inductor current: `i_L` equals L*der(i_L).

5. Resistor current: `i_R` equals R*der(V).
