
Generate a Modelica model named `Damper` for a rotational damper. The model should include a single parameter 'd' of type 'Modelica.SIunits.RotationalDampingConstant' representing the rotational damping constant with units of Newton meters per radian per second. The model should extend 'ModelicaByExample.Components.Rotational.Interfaces.Compliant' and implement an equation for the damping relationship, where the torque 'tau' equals 'd' multiplied by the derivative of 'phi_rel'. This model simulates the damping effect in rotational systems, mitigating oscillations and stabilizing motion.

//modelica
```model Damper
  extends ModelicaByExample.Components.Rotational.Interfaces.Compliant;
  parameter Modelica.SIunits.RotationalDampingConstant d;
equation 
  tau = d * der(phi_rel);
end Damper;
```

This model is a simple representation of a rotational damper. It takes in the relative angle 'phi_rel' and outputs the torque 'tau' which is proportional to the rate of change of this angle. The damping constant 'd' determines the strength of this proportionality.

Please note that this model is a simplified representation of a real-world rotational damper and may not accurately represent the behavior of a real-world damper. For a more accurate model, additional parameters and equations may be necessary.

Also, this model assumes that the rotational system is in a steady state. If the system is not in a steady state, the model may not accurately represent the behavior of the system.

Finally, this model does not consider the direction of rotation. If the direction of rotation is important, additional logic may be necessary to ensure that the torque is applied in the correct direction.

In summary, this model is a basic representation of a rotational damper and should be used with caution. For more accurate simulations, a more complex model may be necessary.
