
We have a container, and we want to keep the height of the water (`waterLevel`) in the container at a target height (`target_height`) of 1 meter. The water height (`waterLevel`) in the pool starts at 0.2m. Use proportional differential control to implement the above control system (defining the height error as `error`, and splitting the logic into proportional control `Pcontrol` and differential control `Dcontrol`) and simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time, where the proportional coefficient (`Kp`) is 1 and the differential coefficient (`Kd`) is 0.1. Please help me implement a modelica model named `PDController`.

//modelica
```model PDController
  parameter Real Kp = 1 "Proportional coefficient";
  parameter Real Kd = 0.1 "Differential coefficient";
  parameter Real target_height = 1 "Target height";
  parameter Real initial_waterLevel = 0.2 "Initial water level";
  Real waterLevel(start = initial_waterLevel) "Water level";
  Real error "Height error";
  Real Pcontrol "Proportional control";
  Real Dcontrol "Differential control";
  Real derivative "Derivative of water level";
initial equation
  error = target_height - initial_waterLevel;
equation
  Pcontrol = Kp * error;
  der(waterLevel) = Pcontrol + Kd * derivative;
  derivative = der(waterLevel);
end PDController;
```

The `initial_waterLevel` should be 0.2, `target_height` should be 1, `Kp` should be 1, and `Kd` should be 0.1. The `waterLevel` should start at 0.2, and the `error` should be the difference between the `target_height` and the `initial_waterLevel`. The `Pcontrol` should be the product of `Kp` and `error`. The derivative of `waterLevel` should be the sum of `Pcontrol` and the product of `Kd` and `derivative`. The `derivative` should be the derivative of `waterLevel`.

Please note that the `waterLevel` should increase over time to reach the `target_height` due to the proportional control, and the rate of increase should decrease over time due to the differential control.
