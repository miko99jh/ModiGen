
We have a reservoir, and we want to keep the height of the water (`waterLevel`) in the reservoir at a target height (`target_height`) of 1m. The water height (`waterLevel`) in the pool is 0.2m at the beginning. Please use proportional control to implement the above control system (defining the altitude error as `error` and the control signal as `control`) and simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time with a proportional factor (`Kp`) of 1. Please help me implement a modelica model named `PController`.

//modelica
```model PController
  parameter Real target_height=1 "Target height of water in reservoir";
  parameter Real Kp=1 "Proportional gain";
  Real waterLevel(start=0.2) "Water level in reservoir";
  Real error "Altitude error";
  Real control "Control signal";
initial equation
  error = target_height - waterLevel;
equation
  control = Kp * error;
  der(waterLevel) = control;
end PController;
```

The `der(waterLevel)` equation is used to model the change in water level over time. The control signal is used to adjust the change in water level. The proportional control system is used to maintain the water level at the target height. The proportional gain (`Kp`) is used to adjust the control signal.

Please note that this model assumes that the water level in the reservoir changes instantaneously according to the control signal. In reality, the change in water level would be slower due to factors such as the volume of the reservoir and the rate at which water can enter or leave the reservoir.

This model can be used to simulate the change in water level in the reservoir over time. The target height of the water in the reservoir, the proportional gain, and the initial water level can be adjusted as needed.
