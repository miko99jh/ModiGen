Use an initial equation to set `T` to `T0`. 20 seconds after the start of the simulation, the ambient temperature suddenly dropped to 0°C.

```model NewtonCoolingDynamic
  parameter Real T0 = 100 "Initial temperature of water";
  parameter Real T_inf(start=25) = 0 "Ambient temperature";

  end when;
end NewtonCoolingDynamic;
```

The specific heat `c_p` is 4200 J/(kg.°C), and the convection cooling coefficient `h` is 767 W/(m2.°C), so the heat transfer rate is `h*A*(T-T_inf)/c_p`. The temperature derivative `der(T)` is equal to the negative of this heat transfer rate, which is the equation that describes the temperature change of the water.

The model is initialized with `T` set to `T0`, which is the initial temperature of the water. The model is solved for `T` as a function of time, starting from the initial condition `T(0) = T0`. The temperature `T` decreases over time as the water cools according to Newton's law of cooling. The temperature `T` is plotted as a function of time to visualize the cooling process.
