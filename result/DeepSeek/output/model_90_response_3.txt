
We have a reservoir, and we want to keep the height of the water (`waterLevel`) in the reservoir at a target height (`target_height`) of 1m. The water height (`waterLevel`) in the pool is 0.2m at the beginning. Please use proportional control to implement the above control system (defining the altitude error as `error` and the control signal as `control`) and simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time with a proportional factor (`Kp`) of 1. Please help me implement a modelica model named `PController`.

//modelica
```model PController
  parameter Real target_height = 1 "target height of water in the reservoir";
  parameter Real waterLevel(start=0.2) "current water height in the reservoir";
  parameter Real Kp = 1 "proportional control factor";
  Real error "altitude error";
  Real control "control signal";
equation
  // Implement the proportional control system here
  error = target_height - waterLevel;
  control = Kp * error;
  der(waterLevel) = control;
end PController;
```

This model implements a proportional control system to maintain the water level in the reservoir at a target height of 1m. The current water level is initially set at 0.2m. The proportional control factor is set to 1. The altitude error is calculated as the difference between the target height and the current water level. The control signal is then calculated by multiplying the proportional factor by the error. The change in water level over time is then determined by the control signal.

The `der(waterLevel) = control;` equation means that the rate of change of the water level is equal to the control signal, which means the water level will increase or decrease at a rate determined by the control signal.

In this model, the proportional control factor (`Kp`) is set to 1, which means the control signal will be equal to the altitude error. If the altitude error is positive, the control signal will be positive and the water level will increase. If the altitude error is negative, the control signal will be negative and the water level will decrease.

The model will simulate the change in water level over time based on the control signal. The water level will approach the target height of 1m as the error becomes smaller and the control signal becomes smaller.

Please note that this model does not consider the dynamics of the system and does not take into account the resistance of the water to change its height. In a real-world scenario, the water level would not change instantaneously but would take some time to reach the target height due to the resistance of the water.

Also, the model does not consider the constraints of the system. For example, the water level in the reservoir cannot be negative or greater than the maximum height of the reservoir. In a real-world scenario, these constraints would need to be taken into account to ensure the safety and efficiency of the system.

Lastly, the model does not consider the cost or the energy required to maintain the water level. In a real-world scenario, the cost of maintaining the water level would need to be taken into account to ensure the profitability of the system.
