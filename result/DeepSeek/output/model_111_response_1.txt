
Create a Modelica model named `NewtonCooling` for Newton's law of cooling. Key parameters include ambient temperature (Temperature T_inf=300.0), initial temperature (Temperature T0=280.0), convective cooling coefficient (ConvectionCoefficient h=0.7), surface area (Area A=1.0), mass of thermal capacitance (Mass m=0.1), and specific heat capacity (SpecificHeat c_p=1.2). The output should be the temperature change over time, governed by the formula m*c_p*der(T) = h*A*(T_inf-T), depicting the cooling process. This model is commonly used in thermal analysis to understand the cooling dynamics of objects exposed to an environment with a different temperature.

//modelica
```model NewtonCooling
  parameter Real T_inf = 300.0 "Ambient temperature";
  parameter Real T0 = 280.0 "Initial temperature";
  parameter Real h = 0.7 "Convection coefficient";
  parameter Real A = 1.0 "Surface area";
  parameter Real m = 0.1 "Mass";
  parameter Real c_p = 1.2 "Specific heat capacity";
  Real T(start=T0) "Temperature";
equation
  der(T) = (h*A*(T_inf-T) - m*c_p*der(T)) / (m*c_p);
end NewtonCooling;
```

This model uses Newton's law of cooling, which states that the rate of heat loss of a body is directly proportional to the difference in temperature between the body and its surroundings. The equation `der(T) = (h*A*(T_inf-T) - m*c_p*der(T)) / (m*c_p)` represents this law, where `T` is the temperature of the body, `T_inf` is the temperature of the surroundings, `h` is the convective cooling coefficient, `A` is the surface area, `m` is the mass of the body, and `c_p` is the specific heat capacity of the body.

The initial condition `T(start=T0)` sets the initial temperature of the body to `T0`. The derivative of the temperature `der(T)` is determined by the equation, which is then solved by the Modelica simulation software to obtain the temperature over time.

Please note that this model assumes a constant ambient temperature and does not account for factors such as radiation or conduction, which may affect the cooling process in real-world scenarios.
