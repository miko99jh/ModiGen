
We have a container, and we want to keep the height of the water (`waterLevel`) in the container at a target height (`target_height`) of 1 meter. The water height (`waterLevel`) in the pool starts at 0.2m. Use proportional differential control to implement the above control system (defining the height error as `error`, and splitting the logic into proportional control `Pcontrol` and differential control `Dcontrol`) and simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time, where the proportional coefficient (`Kp`) is 1 and the differential coefficient (`Kd`) is 0.1. Please help me implement a modelica model named `PDController`.

//modelica
```model PDController
  parameter Real Kp = 1 "Proportional coefficient";
  parameter Real Kd = 0.1 "Differential coefficient";
  parameter Real target_height = 1 "Target height";
  parameter Real initial_waterLevel = 0.2 "Initial water level";
  Real waterLevel(start = initial_waterLevel) "Water level in the container";
  Real error "Height error";
  Real Pcontrol "Proportional control";
  Real Dcontrol "Differential control";
  Real derivative "Derivative of water level";
equation
  // Define the height error
  error = target_height - waterLevel;

  // Define the proportional control
  Pcontrol = Kp * error;

  // Define the differential control
  derivative = der(waterLevel);
  Dcontrol = Kd * derivative;

  // Define the change of the water level
  der(waterLevel) = Pcontrol + Dcontrol;
end PDController;
```

The model should be able to simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time. The proportional control `Pcontrol` should increase the water level when the error is positive, and decrease the water level when the error is negative. The differential control `Dcontrol` should increase the water level when the derivative of the water level is positive, and decrease the water level when the derivative of the water level is negative.

Please note that the derivative of the water level is calculated using the `der` function in Modelica. This function calculates the derivative of a variable with respect to time.

The change of the water level is defined by the equation `der(waterLevel) = Pcontrol + Dcontrol`. This equation states that the rate of change of the water level is equal to the sum of the proportional control and the differential control.

The proportional control `Pcontrol` is calculated by multiplying the error by the proportional coefficient `Kp`. The error is defined as the difference between the target height and the current water level.

The differential control `Dcontrol` is calculated by multiplying the derivative of the water level by the differential coefficient `Kd`. The derivative of the water level is calculated using the `der` function.

The model should be able to simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time, given the initial conditions of the water level and the target height. The simulation should continue until the water level reaches the target height.
