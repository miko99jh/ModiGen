
We have a container, and we want to keep the height of the water (`waterLevel`) in the container at a target height (`target_height`) of 1 meter. The water height (`waterLevel`) in the pool starts at 0.2m, and the pool will leak 0.1m per unit time. Use proportional integral control to implement the above control system (defining height error as `error`, and splitting the logic into proportional control `Pcontrol`, which handles the leak, and integral control `Icontrol`) and simulate the change of the water level over time, where the proportional coefficient (`Kp`) is 1 and the integral coefficient (`Ki`) is 0.5. Please help me implement a modelica model named `PIController`.

//modelica
```model PIController
  parameter Real waterLevel(min=0) = 0.2; //initial water level
  parameter Real target_height = 1; //target height
  parameter Real Kp = 1; //proportional coefficient
  parameter Real Ki = 0.5; //integral coefficient
  Real error; //height error
  Real Pcontrol; //proportional control
  Real Icontrol; //integral control
  Real waterLevel_dot; //derivative of water level
initial equation
  error = target_height - waterLevel;
equation
  //define the proportional control
  Pcontrol = Kp * error;
  //define the integral control
  Icontrol = Ki * integral(error, 0);
  //define the derivative of water level
  waterLevel_dot = Pcontrol + Icontrol - 0.1; //leak rate is 0.1m/s
  //define the differential equation of water level
  der(waterLevel) = waterLevel_dot;
end PIController;
```

The model above is a simple model of a PI controller. It calculates the error between the current water level and the target height, and then uses the proportional and integral control to adjust the water level. The proportional control is directly proportional to the error, and the integral control is the integral of the error. The derivative of the water level is calculated by subtracting the leak rate from the sum of the proportional and integral control. Finally, the differential equation of the water level is solved to simulate the change of the water level over time.

Please note that the integral control is initialized to 0, and the integral is calculated over the error. The integral control is used to reduce the steady-state error of the system. The proportional control is used to quickly respond to changes in the error. The sum of the proportional and integral control is used to adjust the water level. The derivative of the water level is used to simulate the leak in the pool. The differential equation of the water level is used to simulate the change of the water level over time.
