
Suppose we have a simple vibration system consisting of a spring, a damper, and a mass (`m`) that moves in a vertical direction without external forces (`F0` = 0). The spring coefficient (`k`) is 1, the damping coefficient (`c`) is 0.1, and the particle mass (`m`) is 1. Please help me implement the Modelica model named `SpringMassDamper` corresponding to the above system. Define variables for displacement (`x`, starting at 1), velocity (`v`), and the resultant force applied to the mass (`F`). The equations should describe the resultant force (`F`) based on the spring and damping effects, and use Newton's second law to relate this force to the derivative of velocity, while also defining the derivative of displacement.

//modelica
```model SpringMassDamper
  parameter Real m = 1 "Mass";
  parameter Real k = 1 "Spring coefficient";
  parameter Real c = 0.1 "Damping coefficient";
  Real x(start=1) "Displacement";
  Real v "Velocity";
  Real F "Resultant force";
initial equation
  v = 0;
equation
  F = k*x + c*v;
  m*der(v) = F;
  der(x) = v;
end SpringMassDamper;
```

This Modelica model describes a simple spring-mass-damper system. The model has three parameters: `m` (mass), `k` (spring coefficient), and `c` (damping coefficient). The model has three variables: `x` (displacement), `v` (velocity), and `F` (resultant force). The equations describe the relationship between these variables. The resultant force `F` is the sum of the force due to the spring (`k*x`) and the damping force (`c*v`). The acceleration of the mass is given by Newton's second law, which states that the force on an object is equal to its mass times its acceleration. The velocity and displacement are related by their derivatives.

Note: The initial values for `v` and `x` are set to 0 and 1 respectively.

This model can be used to simulate the behavior of a spring-mass-damper system. The simulation results can be used to analyze the system's response to different inputs, such as changes in the displacement or velocity of the mass, or changes in the parameters of the system.

Please note that this is a simplified model and does not account for external forces or other potential sources of energy loss in the system. For a more accurate model, additional parameters and equations would need to be included.
