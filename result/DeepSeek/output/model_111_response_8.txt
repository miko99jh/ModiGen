
Create a Modelica model named `NewtonCooling` for Newton's law of cooling. Key parameters include ambient temperature (Temperature T_inf=300.0), initial temperature (Temperature T0=280.0), convective cooling coefficient (ConvectionCoefficient h=0.7), surface area (Area A=1.0), mass of thermal capacitance (Mass m=0.1), and specific heat capacity (SpecificHeat c_p=1.2). The output should be the temperature change over time, governed by the formula m*c_p*der(T) = h*A*(T_inf-T), depicting the cooling process. This model is commonly used in thermal analysis to understand the cooling dynamics of objects exposed to an environment with a different temperature.

//modelica
```model NewtonCooling
  parameter Real T_inf = 300.0 "Ambient temperature";
  parameter Real T0 = 280.0 "Initial temperature";
  parameter Real h = 0.7 "Convection coefficient";
  parameter Real A = 1.0 "Surface area";
  parameter Real m = 0.1 "Mass";
  parameter Real c_p = 1.2 "Specific heat capacity";
  Real T(start=T0) "Temperature";
equation
  der(T) = (h*A*(T_inf-T) - m*c_p*T)/m/c_p;
end NewtonCooling;```

This Modelica model uses Newton's law of cooling to calculate the temperature change of an object over time. The law states that the rate of heat loss of a body is directly proportional to the difference in temperatures between the body and its surroundings. The model takes into account the ambient temperature, initial temperature, convective cooling coefficient, surface area, mass of the object, and specific heat capacity. The temperature change is then calculated using this information.

The equation `der(T) = (h*A*(T_inf-T) - m*c_p*T)/m/c_p;` is the core of the model, which represents the rate of change of temperature (dT/dt) as a function of the heat loss (h*A*(T_inf-T)) and the heat gain (m*c_p*T). The heat loss is proportional to the difference in temperature between the object and the ambient temperature, while the heat gain is proportional to the current temperature. The model then uses these two terms to calculate the rate of temperature change.

The model starts with an initial temperature T0 and uses the parameters to calculate the temperature over time. The model can be used to simulate the cooling process of various objects under different conditions.
