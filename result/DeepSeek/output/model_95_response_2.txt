
Suppose we have a simple vibration system consisting of a spring, a damper, and a mass (`m`) that moves in a vertical direction without external forces (`F0` = 0). The spring coefficient (`k`) is 1, the damping coefficient (`c`) is 0.1, and the particle mass (`m`) is 1. Please help me implement the Modelica model named `SpringMassDamper` corresponding to the above system. Define variables for displacement (`x`, starting at 1), velocity (`v`), and the resultant force applied to the mass (`F`). The equations should describe the resultant force (`F`) based on the spring and damping effects, and use Newton's second law to relate this force to the derivative of velocity, while also defining the derivative of displacement.

//modelica
```model SpringMassDamper
  parameter Real k = 1 "Spring coefficient";
  parameter Real c = 0.1 "Damping coefficient";
  parameter Real m = 1 "Particle mass";
  Real x(start = 1) "Displacement";
  Real v "Velocity";
  Real F "Resultant force applied to the mass";
equation
  // Define the resultant force F based on the spring and damping effects
  F = k*x + c*v;
  // Use Newton's second law to relate this force to the derivative of velocity
  m*der(v) = F;
  // Define the derivative of displacement
  der(x) = v;
end SpringMassDamper;```

This Modelica model describes a simple spring-mass-damper system. The spring force is given by `k*x`, where `x` is the displacement of the mass from the equilibrium position, and `k` is the spring constant. The damping force is given by `c*v`, where `v` is the velocity of the mass, and `c` is the damping coefficient. The resultant force `F` on the mass is the sum of these two forces. Newton's second law of motion states that the force on a mass is equal to its mass times its acceleration, so `F = m*a`, where `a` is the acceleration of the mass. Rearranging this equation gives `m*der(v) = F`, which is the equation for the acceleration of the mass. Finally, the equation `der(x) = v` describes how the velocity changes with time, which is the derivative of the displacement.

This model can be used in a simulation environment that supports Modelica to simulate the motion of the mass under the influence of the spring and damping forces.

Note: The initial conditions for the displacement and velocity are set to 1 and 0 respectively, but these can be changed as needed for different simulations.
