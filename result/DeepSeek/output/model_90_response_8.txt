
We have a reservoir, and we want to keep the height of the water (`waterLevel`) in the reservoir at a target height (`target_height`) of 1m. The water height (`waterLevel`) in the pool is 0.2m at the beginning. Please use proportional control to implement the above control system (defining the altitude error as `error` and the control signal as `control`) and simulate the change of the water level in the pool over time with a proportional factor (`Kp`) of 1. Please help me implement a modelica model named `PController`.

//modelica
```model PController
  parameter Real target_height = 1 "Target water level in reservoir";
  parameter Real Kp = 1 "Proportional control factor";
  Real waterLevel(start = 0.2) "Current water level in reservoir";
  Real error "Error in water level";
  Real control "Control signal";
equation
  error = target_height - waterLevel;
  control = Kp * error;
  der(waterLevel) = control;
end PController;
```

This model uses a proportional controller to maintain the water level in the reservoir at the target height. The error is calculated as the difference between the target height and the current water level. The control signal is then calculated by multiplying the error by the proportional control factor. The change in the water level is then determined by the control signal.

This model assumes that the water level in the reservoir can change instantaneously according to the control signal. In reality, this might not be the case due to factors such as the volume of water in the reservoir and the time it takes for the water to flow into or out of the reservoir. However, for simplicity, this model assumes that these factors are not considered.

Please note that the model does not include any initial or boundary conditions, and it assumes that the water level in the reservoir can start at any value and that the control signal can be any real number. In a real-world application, these conditions would need to be considered.

This model can be used to simulate the behavior of a proportional controller in a water level control system. The parameters `target_height` and `Kp` can be adjusted to simulate different target water levels and control factors. The `waterLevel` can be observed over time to see how the system responds to the control signal.
